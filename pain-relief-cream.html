What is a Topical Pain Cream, and why should you buy one?

A topical cream is one that is applied to the skin directly and absorbs into targeted areas. In that way it is a localized pain relief compared with opiods or nerve medications which alter brain chemistry to prevent pain signals from reaching the brain. Topicals arent new by any means, but modern active ingredients in topicals are more available and often more potent, concentrated, and calculated for specific effects.

So if you have Aspirin or Tylenol, why would you want a cream? Simple, your liver will thank you for treating the affected areas rather than a blanket application of a numbing effect. Its safer, faster, and simpler to use topicals than to rely on prolongued use of pills to treat pain. Plus topicals don't create any brain fog so you can go about your day after using them.

Additionally, some of these topicals contain more than just numbing agents giving you temporary relief, some can also provide long-term relief and a faster recovery time.

Important Topical Pain Cream Features

As a shopper, it can be difficult to know what to look for in a product. It seems as though each industry has it's own buzzwords and features designed to confuse. But which features and phrases are important to know when it comes to pain creams? Below we've compiled a short list of the key features to look for when shopping for pain relief creams.

Application

There are a variety of options available for application of key active ingredients to help alleviate pain through topical products. From gels and creams, to sprays and wipes, to powders and lotions. Ultimately it will come down to a personal preference regarding the comfort and ease of application to the targeted areas. In this article we've largely addressed creams since they seem to be the easiest to concentrate on specific areas and have the least greasy after effects compared with gels and oils and greater absorption compared with powders and aerosol sprays.

Time from Application to Relief

The time it takes for relief to occur will vary based on individual metabolic function in absorbing and processing active ingredients and on the type of active ingredient. Often, products will layer relief effects such as counterirritant's for immediate surface level relief as a stop gap between application and the more significant effects of anti-inflammartory agents.

Anti-inflammatory

Bodies undergo normal degrading over time and as a result of our musculature being pushed beyond it's current capacity. Luckily our bodies are a marvel capable of incredible feats of regeneration. One hinderence to that process is a restriction in bloodflow as a result of swelling. Less bloodflow can mean a slower recovery. The state of localized swelling is referred to as Inflammation or an inflammartory response. When blood loss is not a concern, Inflammation can only serve to slow recovery and prolong pain. The more inflammation the body has, the more likely it is to experience pain, especially around joints which are sensative due to damage from age or injury. Many pain creams will employ anti-inflammatory agents to help alleviate pain long term for injuries and as a preventative for age related ailments. It is important to look for products that are specifically designed to help alleviate pain associated with Inflammation for the maximum long term effects.

Allergy Response

Pain creams often contain a variety of proprietary differentiating ingredients which offer extra benefits or who work in conjunction with the actives. In some cases, users of the product will trigger an allergic response which may result in rashes or other skin related symptoms. This is why it is also very important to look for products that Dermatologist Tested. Give yourself peace of mind by knowing that the products you use on your skin are safe and effective.

Counterirritant

The human nervous system and brain is capable of interpreting large numbers of signals but it creates a weighting system based on the intensity of those signals and that weighting system is the basis of counterirritant inclusion in pain cream products. The simple idea is that your body will prioritize the signal of a new irritant over the existing signal, making your perception shift regarding previous signal's severity. In other words, pain is a nerve signal telling your brain that your body is injured and needs special consideration to ensure your survival. But those signals are not always an accurate representation of the danger the signal represents. Or in other cases the signal has no real value because there is no fixing the cause of the signal. It is in these cases where the counterirritant is the most effective. Think of an alarm clock that never turns off, even well after you are awake! The alarm clock is the pain signal and the counterirritant lets you hit snooze, often with much more tolerable sensations like heat and cold.

Trial Period

While the combination of anti-inflammartory agents and counterirritants are a great relief to many people, there is a risk of the product not working as intended. The trial period is the time period during which the product can be tested to determine if it is a real solution to your particular ailments. If the product is not providing relief, you want to be able to recoup the cost and move on to the next stage in your pain management journey. That's why it is worth prioritizing products which include refunds and satisfaction guarantees. There are many products with this model, varying from 30 day trials, to Lifetime Guarantees. There are enough options in that model, with similar potency active ingredients, that you shouldn't bother risking products which do not offer some version of a full refund if you are dissatisfied with the product.

Animal Testing

Of large concern to many people is the safety of the product. It has long been the practice of companies to test on Animals. But in the last few decades there has been a concerted effort to phase out the practice for more human testing practices among consenting participants. We believe it is important to acknowledge that the testing of animals is a serious issue and that it is not a safe, humane, or just practice. If this issue is important to you, we highly recommend looking for evidence that the company you purchase from is verified to be Cruelty-Free. While it does require extra effort which increases the production costs of a product, we think it is worth the small premium to support the cause of protecting our furry friends.

Tips

Whether you are new to pain relief creams or have used them after every sporting event or long day on your feet, we think its helpful to review the following tips to help you get the most out of your Topical Pain Cream.

  • Clean Application Site. Topical absorption into the skin can be hindered by dirt and oils that built up naturally over time. We recommend giving your body the best chance to absorb the active ingredients to see the fastest and strongest results. Avoid using rough exfoliant cleansers to prevent microabrasions on the skin prior to application, Ouch! Try using a baby wipe or alcohol wipe on the primary areas before applying any creams.
  • Use a Clean Applicator. Some products offer helpful accessories which let you apply the creams to hard to reach places like the bottoms of the feet and all along the back. It is important to wash cloth applicators to maintain them over time and to prevent aggitating skin conditions
  • Avoid Application On Skin with Rashes or Open Wounds. Menthol and other cooling actives can cause stinging and discomfort on cuts, sores, rashes, or open wounds. It is best to avoid these areas for topical application.
  • Allow Oxygenation. Try to allow the application site to breathe and dry after rubbing in thoroughly. It is important to permit optimal absorption
  • Follow Directions for Use. Products can differ greatly in their composition. While most topical products will have similar instructions, you should always look at the directions for use to determine quantity of the product to use based on it's strength and as well whether there are any warnings about interactions. When questioning if a product is right for your needs, consult your doctor first.
  • Avoid Touching Eye Area After Use. It's very common for people to touch their face unconciously, whether because of an itch or a mannerism. But after applying a topical cream containing cooling or heating elements, it can be dangerous and painful in contact with your eyes. In the same way you wouldn't want to get Shampoo in your eye, you don't want pain cream in it either. We've yet to find a no-more-tears pain cream equivelant, so if your pain is in your hands, you should avoid touching your eyes for at least 30 minutes after applying. And if your pain is localized elsewhere, then strongly consider washing your hands thoroughly after you finish the application.
Pain can make routine exercise a difficult experience, but exercise is increasingly important for muscle and bone health as we age. Pain Creams are a great way to lessen the pain associated with routine healthy exercise. Stretch well after a small warm up, exercise to your target heart rate, take a post-work out shower, and apply pain cream for a painless post workout recouperation period.

Topical Pain Cream Prices

When looking at the cost of these products, it is important to standardize the cost of the product by comparing like with like. This means we don't include other forms of application like sprays or roll-ons which will have different costs to their production than creams.

It is also important to note the variety of sizes that brands offer as well as their bulk purchase discount options. If you are a consumer of these creams regularly it may be your best bet to buy in bulk at a discount every 3-6 months rather than order single use containers or the smallest size of bottle.

There are also often seasonal discounts available from different brands that we can't account for since they are limited time and therefore not reflective of the most common customer experience, though we do highly recommend subscribing to email lists as a free way to get discount codes if you are a routine buyer.

In comparing like with like, the next factor to standardize is the potency per oz of the active ingredients, especially when those ingredients are in common like we often find with Menthol's. There can be a deceptive appeal to a larger container despite competitors offering the same type of active ingredient in higher doses but smaller containers. These ingredient costs are quite reflective in the available offerings. We prefer to get more of the good stuff per oz and use smaller amounts than to lather on more and more to get the same effect.

And one final complication in the cost analysis is how the active ingredient is sourced. It is often more cost effective for a producer of these products to source the active ingredient from a synthetic source. In the case of Pain Creams, the Menthol can be synthesized using a chemical process which involves astringent chemicals like turpintine, things which you would otherwise want to avoid. Given the choice we prefer the premium associated with a natural derivation process. When in doubt, we recommend looking for natural and cruelty-free sources of our desired actives.

FAQ

Q. Why does everyone put Menthol in their creams?

A. Menthol is a great cooling agent for topical applications. It is dermatologist tested and approved to provide counterirritation for fast, if temporary, pain relief.

Q. How often should I be using a pain cream?

A. Most brands will recommend a single application per day, but some may require more applications to provide equivelant relief based on the strength of the product and the severity of the symptoms. If you are worried you are using too much or too often, consult the product label and, ultimately, your Doctor for follow up care as your condition may require consultation.

Q. Is heating and cooling all topical pain creams are good for?

A. No! Not all pain creams are created equal, some will include topical vasodilators to increase bloodflow to the application regions and others will include Anti-inflammatory compounds to reduce swelling at the application site. In some cases there are even compounds which will provide nerve blocking effects to that region. Be careful with this kind by following directions carefully as provided on packaging to ensure safe use of the product on only approved areas of your body.
